# Agree Superfoods Brand System

## Brand direction

Agree Superfoods should feel premium, calm, credible, and contemporary. The visual and verbal system should signal quality and trust first, then wellness and product discovery.

## Suggested logo direction

- Use a compact `AG` monogram for app icons, social avatars, stamps, and favicon contexts.
- Pair the monogram with a clean wordmark: `Agree` on the first line and `Superfoods` as a small supporting label.
- Use rounded corners and strong geometry so the mark feels modern and packaging-ready.
- Avoid busy botanical illustrations or overly spiritual wellness branding.

## Color palette

- Deep Green: `#243A2D`
  - Primary brand anchor, headers, icons, buttons
- Forest Ink: `#132018`
  - High-contrast text and serious trust moments
- Warm Sand: `#FAF7F0`
  - Background wash, whitespace field, premium calm
- Natural Beige: `#F3ECDE`
  - Card surfaces, panels, packaging-inspired sections
- Seed Gold: `#D8C48B`
  - Accent, trust highlights, premium details
- Soft Olive: `#EFF5ED`
  - Supportive backgrounds for badges and chips

## Typography pairing

- Display: `Fraunces`
  - Use for hero headings, section titles, major trust statements
- Sans: `Manrope`
  - Use for paragraphs, navigation, UI, metadata, and forms

## Homepage messaging hierarchy

1. Premium healthy foods brand with trust-first presentation
2. Seeds, teas, makhana, and pantry essentials that fit real daily routines
3. Compliance-aware, business-ready brand positioning
4. Educational content that supports SEO and authority
5. Enquiry-led conversion until ecommerce launches

## Tagline options

- Premium Superfoods for Everyday Rituals
- Pure Pantry Essentials, Presented with Trust
- Seeds, Teas, and Wellness Foods with Clarity
- Everyday Healthy Foods, Elevated
- Trust-Led Superfoods for Modern Households

## Brand voice guidelines

- Sound warm, informed, and disciplined
- Use wellness language, not medical promises
- Prefer clarity over hype
- Avoid disease-curing, treatment, or guaranteed-outcome claims
- Write as a credible consumer brand that is also ready for business conversations
- Keep copy helpful enough to rank and useful enough to convert

## Packaging-inspired UI direction

- Use generous whitespace and quiet surfaces
- Prefer rounded cards over sharp boxes
- Keep shadows soft and expensive-looking
- Use small uppercase labels for trust cues and category markers
- Let product art sit in calm neutral fields rather than loud promotional banners
- Make the interface feel like a premium food catalogue, not a discount marketplace

## CTA wording guidelines

- Use direct, low-friction actions:
  - Explore Products
  - Enquire Now
  - Contact Us
  - Bulk / Wholesale
  - Read More
- Avoid aggressive sales phrasing:
  - no `Buy Now` until ecommerce is live
  - no fake urgency
  - no misleading offer language

## SEO-friendly messaging framework

- Brand pages:
  - focus on quality, sourcing, trust, compliance readiness, and category clarity
- Product pages:
  - focus on ingredient identity, usage ideas, highlights, and realistic consumer context
- Blog pages:
  - focus on informational intent, comparisons, kitchen use, pantry habits, and educational linking
- Compliance pages:
  - focus on professional presentation, business identity, and factual trust signals

## Content guardrails

- Do not write false medical claims
- Do not imply prevention, treatment, or cure of disease
- Do not use manipulative or spam-like SEO copy
- Use descriptive alt text for every image
- Keep metadata unique and page-specific
